# Bhubesi Ventures — Standard Operating Procedures

These SOPs cover how a venture moves from sourced idea to supported, operating entity. For how ideas are logged and evaluated before this point, see [`../../projects/future-ventures/sops.md`](../../projects/future-ventures/sops.md).

## 1. Intake

1. Idea received via any sourcing channel in [`marketing.md`](./marketing.md), logged in [`../../projects/future-ventures/README.md`](../../projects/future-ventures/README.md).
2. Initial screen by [Chief Research Officer](../../ai-agents/workforce/chief-research-officer.md): evidence base, market fit, strategic alignment with [`strategy.md`](./strategy.md).
3. Deal-structure hypothesis formed (internal vs. external, and if external, which structure per [`strategy.md`](./strategy.md)).

## 2. Onboarding a Selected Venture

1. Company formation (if a new legal entity is needed) — coordinated by [Chief Legal Officer](../../ai-agents/workforce/chief-legal-officer.md).
2. Deal terms finalized and signed off by CFO and Chief Legal Officer.
3. Shared-services engagement scoped — which services from [`operations.md`](./operations.md) the venture needs and in what sequence.
4. A named internal sponsor assigned (typically Chief Research Officer or a relevant business-unit seat) to own the relationship through incubation.

## 3. Ongoing Support Delivery

1. Services delivered per [`operations.md`](./operations.md) — strategy, finance, legal, marketing, technology, AI implementation, operations support, investment readiness, grant writing, executive mentoring.
2. Monthly check-in between the venture and its internal sponsor.
3. Quarterly formal review against the venture's own objectives, per [`executive-brain/quarterly-planning-framework.md`](../../executive-brain/quarterly-planning-framework.md).

## 4. Graduation or Exit

1. A venture graduates into a standing business unit or project once it meets the exit criteria set at onboarding (revenue, user, or milestone-based, defined case by case).
2. Alternatively, a venture may be wound down, sold, or spun out — each an Executive Office decision per [`docs/governance.md`](../../docs/governance.md) and Constitution [Article VIII](../../executive-brain/constitution.md#article-viii--dissolution-and-exit).
3. Either outcome is documented via [`templates/decision-log-template.md`](../../templates/decision-log-template.md), not left implicit.

## 5. Escalation

- **Deal structure disagreement:** escalate to CFO and Chief Legal Officer, then CEO if unresolved.
- **Venture underperformance:** escalate to Chief Research Officer and the internal sponsor for a go/no-go review.
- **Resourcing conflict with an existing business unit:** escalate to [COO](../../ai-agents/workforce/coo.md) per [`workflows/decision-making.md`](../../workflows/decision-making.md).
