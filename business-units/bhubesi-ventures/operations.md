# Bhubesi Ventures — Operations

## Shared Services on Offer

Bhubesi Ventures' core operating model is delivering the following as shared services to every venture it supports, rather than each venture building its own:

| Service | Delivered By |
|---|---|
| Business strategy | Business Strategist role, [Chief Research Officer](../../ai-agents/workforce/chief-research-officer.md) |
| Company formation | [Chief Legal Officer](../../ai-agents/workforce/chief-legal-officer.md) |
| Shared finance | [CFO](../../ai-agents/workforce/cfo.md) |
| Shared legal | Chief Legal Officer |
| Marketing | [Chief Marketing Officer](../../ai-agents/workforce/chief-marketing-officer.md) |
| Technology | [CTO](../../ai-agents/workforce/cto.md) |
| AI implementation | CTO, drawing on [`ai-agents/`](../../ai-agents) |
| Operations | [COO](../../ai-agents/workforce/coo.md) |
| Investment readiness | CFO, Chief Research Officer |
| Grant writing | [Grant Writer](../../ai-agents/workforce/grant-writer.md) |
| Executive mentoring | CEO seat and relevant function seats, matched to the venture's needs |

## Org Roles Involved

Every Executive Office seat has a standing role in supporting ventures — this is the point of the shared-services model: a venture doesn't need to hire its own CFO, legal counsel, or marketing lead on day one.

## Operating Rhythm

- **Continuous:** opportunity logging into [Future Ventures](../../projects/future-ventures/README.md).
- **Monthly:** executive portfolio review across all active ventures.
- **Quarterly:** formal strategic review per [`executive-brain/quarterly-planning-framework.md`](../../executive-brain/quarterly-planning-framework.md).

## Systems and Tools

- **CRM** (planned [Bhubesi OS](../../projects/bhubesi-os/README.md) module) — deal-flow and founder relationship pipeline.
- **Project Management** (planned Bhubesi OS module) — tracking each venture's onboarding and support engagement.
- **Finance module** (planned Bhubesi OS module) — deal economics and shared-services cost allocation across ventures.

## Capacity Constraints

Shared-services capacity (a small Executive Office team supporting multiple ventures at once) is the binding constraint on how many ventures can be actively supported at a time — sequencing per [`sops.md`](./sops.md) Section 2 matters more than sourcing volume until this capacity grows.
