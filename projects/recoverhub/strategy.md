# RecoverHUB — Strategy

## Vision

A recovery ecosystem where people rebuilding their lives after substance use disorder and addiction have structured, dignified access to the resources, relationships, and opportunities that make recovery durable — not just sobriety, but reintegration into family, work, and community.

## Problem

Recovery support in the markets RecoverHUB serves is fragmented: rehabilitation ends at discharge, with little structured support for what follows — employment, financial stability, family repair, and community belonging. Relapse risk is highest exactly where support drops off. RecoverHUB exists to hold that gap.

## Who This Is For

- **Primary users:** individuals in recovery, at any stage from early treatment through long-term maintenance.
- **Secondary users (partners and referral/delivery channels):** rehabilitation centres, NGOs, employers, government departments, correctional services, educational institutions, and healthcare providers — organizations that refer individuals in, co-deliver programming, or fund access.

## Positioning

RecoverHUB is not a treatment provider — it is the connective layer *after* and *around* treatment: programme design, media/education content, training, and (in time) coaching, delivered through and alongside the institutions people in recovery already touch.

## Strategic Horizon

Maps to Horizon 1 (Foundation) in [`executive-brain/strategy-10-year.md`](../../executive-brain/strategy-10-year.md):

- **Now (Phase 1, ~12 months):** establish brand, core programme design, and 1–3 anchor partnerships (a rehabilitation centre, an employer or government department, and an NGO) to prove the model with a real cohort.
- **Next:** expand B2B/B2G partnerships and grant funding once the Phase 1 cohort produces evidence of outcomes; introduce training/workshop revenue.
- **Later:** premium recovery coaching as a direct-to-individual offering, once the institutional side is generating durable revenue and credibility.

## Success Looks Like

- At least one rehabilitation centre or government/employer partner actively referring and co-delivering with RecoverHUB by the end of Phase 1.
- A documented outcome measure for participants (e.g., employment status, relapse rate, or a validated wellbeing measure) — the evidence base that justifies expansion.
