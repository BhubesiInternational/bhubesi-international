# The Chairman — Strategy

## Vision

A feature documentary, produced to international festival and streaming standards, that uses one person's journey to explore leadership, resilience, influence, and legacy in a way that resonates beyond the subject's own circle — the flagship proof point for Bhubesi Media's production quality.

## Narrative Approach

A human-centred story: the film follows "The Chairman's" personal and professional journey, using that specific life as the lens for universal themes (leadership under pressure, resilience through setback, the weight and use of influence, what legacy actually means once built). The narrative approach favors intimacy and access over hagiography — credibility with festival and streaming audiences depends on the film being honest, not promotional.

## Format

Primary: feature documentary. Secondary option under evaluation: expansion into a limited documentary series if the access and material support a longer arc — this is a scope decision to be finalized in the project brief, not assumed.

## Distribution Strategy

Layered, in priority order:

1. **International film festivals** — establishes critical credibility and awards positioning before wider release.
2. **Streaming platforms** — primary revenue and reach vehicle post-festival run.
3. **Broadcast television** — secondary/territorial distribution.
4. **Educational licensing** — long-tail revenue and impact distribution (universities, leadership programmes), consistent with the film's themes.

## Strategic Horizon

Maps to Horizon 1 (Foundation) in [`executive-brain/strategy-10-year.md`](../../executive-brain/strategy-10-year.md) — this is one of the flagship proof points Horizon 1 explicitly calls for from Bhubesi Media.

- **Now (Development):** research, story development, subject access agreements, and funding preparation.
- **Next (Production):** principal photography, guided by the approved project brief.
- **Later (Post/Distribution):** edit, festival submission strategy, then platform/broadcast/educational licensing per the distribution strategy above.

- Subject access and rights fully secured before production begins (see [`sops.md`](./sops.md)).
- Festival acceptance at a recognized international festival.
- A distribution deal (streaming, broadcast, or both) secured post-festival run.
