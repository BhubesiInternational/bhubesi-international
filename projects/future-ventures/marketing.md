# Future Ventures — Marketing (Idea Sourcing)

This document covers how ideas reach the pipeline. For how a graduated venture is marketed externally once it launches, see that venture's own `marketing.md`.

## Who Can Propose an Idea

Anyone at Bhubesi International — no gatekeeping at the intake stage (see [`sops.md`](./sops.md) Section 1). This is deliberate: the cost of logging an idea should be near zero, since evaluation (not intake) is where rigor is applied.

## External Sourcing Channels

Consistent with [`../../business-units/bhubesi-ventures/marketing.md`](../../business-units/bhubesi-ventures/marketing.md):

- External founders.
- Universities.
- Government innovation programmes.
- Corporate partnerships.

## Internal Framing

Internally, Future Ventures should be positioned as "where good ideas go to get real resourcing" — not a graveyard for ideas nobody wants to fund. A pipeline that only ever accumulates and never graduates or declines anything will stop attracting serious proposals.

## What Makes the Current Pipeline List Notable

The current pipeline (Bhubesi AI, Bhubesi Consulting, Bhubesi Studios, Bhubesi Academy, Bhubesi Capital, Bhubesi Foundation, future SaaS products, creative technology initiatives — see [`README.md`](./README.md)) reads as a set of **sibling entities to Bhubesi International's existing business units**, not incremental product ideas. Several (Bhubesi Studios, Bhubesi Capital) closely mirror existing units (Bhubesi Media/Creative, Bhubesi Ventures itself) — evaluation should explicitly address whether each is a genuinely new venture or better modeled as an expansion of an existing business unit, per [`strategy.md`](./strategy.md)'s "systems compound" pillar.

## Cadence

Idea sourcing is continuous; see [`README.md`](./README.md) for the review cadence (continuous logging, monthly portfolio review, quarterly strategic review).
